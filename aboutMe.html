Recently, I got into the great big world of keyboards, and I must admit it is definitely an interest that's unique with a phenomoenal international community behind it. As someone who uses the computer very often, I think it's important to get a piece of equipment that is perfectly suited towards your specific taste/preference. Every keyboard has a distinct set of features along with a unique typing experience, so I'm always on the lookout for new intruiging releases. I love the fact that mechanical keyboards in particular are so highly customizable, and some really unqiue and interesting designs have come out of it. I haven't tried building my own yet, but I would love one day. Right now I'm hoping to acquire a board that is hot-swappable so I can try out all the exotic switch types that are out there. Right now, my mechanical collection consists of a Fnatic miniStreak (Cherry mx silent reds), an XG KB-2128 (outemu blues).

Device Modding/Repair

Although somewhat of a weird niche, I enjoy fixing/tinkering with all sorts of electronic devices, most notably PS4 controllers. I have replaced the cracked screen of a 2012 Google Nexus 7 as well as numerous PS4 controller fixes/customizations. The first memory I have of fixing controllers was in grade 10, when I replaced the loose micro USB charging port on the controller. I then went in again and replaced the faulty springs on the triggers. After a while I replaced the analog sticks and buttons with some metal golden pieces. At the time of writing, I am waiting for the shipment of a membrane circuit film, as I plan on fixing a broken controller that I bought second hand. In my opinion, the customization/fixing of controllers is just as much fun as playing the games themselves.
